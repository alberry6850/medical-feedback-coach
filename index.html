Feedback Best Practices

🎯 Be Specific

Use concrete examples and behaviors rather than generalizations. Say "I noticed you were 20 minutes late" not "You're always late."

👂 Listen First

Ask open-ended questions to understand the student's perspective. This builds rapport and shows you value their input.

🤝 Use I-Statements

Frame feedback from your perspective: "I've observed..." or "I'm concerned about..." rather than accusatory language.

🎁 Balance with Strengths

Acknowledge what the student does well before diving into challenges. This creates psychological safety.

🚀 Focus on Growth

Frame feedback as an opportunity for development. Offer support and resources to help the student improve.

✅ Collaboratively Plan

Work together to identify concrete next steps and follow-up points. Share ownership of the improvement plan.

Key Takeaways

  • Effective feedback is specific, timely, and balanced
  • Listening and understanding the student's perspective is crucial
  • Frame challenges as growth opportunities, not criticisms
  • Collaborate on solutions and follow-up plans
  • Consistent, supportive feedback promotes professional development
